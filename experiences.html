Projects

Michigan Mars Rover Team - Ann Arbor, MI

June 2019 - present

I joined the Michigan Mars Rover Team (MRover) in the fall of 2017. MRover is a student run team comprised of individuals across various disciplines at the University of Michigan that designs, builds, and tests a mars rover for competition at the University Rover Challenge every summer. During my first year on MRover, I joined the Autonomous Navigation subteam where I, along with two other members, was tasked with developing the initial state machine that executed all navigation logic for autonomous mode on the rover. The main goal was to develop code to autonomously drive the rover between marked waypoints avoiding any obstacles in our path. This was an extremely challenging project for us, but, after many iterations, we developed a functional program. While autonomy mode worked extremely well in our custom built simulator, the integration with the computer vision system and odometry sensors limited our performance at competition. Thus, I turned our focus for the following year to improving the robustness of the system in the real world despite having imperfect inputs. We gutted our search algorithm and redesigned it to work in a way that reduced false-negatives, by adding strategic pauses, and handled false-positive without discarding previous work done. I also designed a sensor fusion algorithm to increase the accuracy of our location and heading estimates by using all sensor data to calculate multiple estimates and weigh them based on our confidence in each estimate. While I have maintained the current version of our similator, I am currently working with a group of members to develop the next iteration which will add abilities such as simulating real world noise and running automated test cases. I am also working with a group to add ultra-sonic and time-of-flight sensors to the rover to add an additional layer of object detection to the rover and add support for detecting steep dropoffs in our path of travel.

Technical skills: C++, Python, Git, GitHub
